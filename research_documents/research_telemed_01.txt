Telemedicine platforms deliver synchronous and asynchronous clinical consultations through HIPAA-compliant video conferencing, secure patient messaging portals, and store-and-forward diagnostic workflows. Real-time teleconsultations connect patients with physicians via encrypted WebRTC video streams, enabling visual assessment, medication management, and behavioral health therapy sessions without in-person visits. Asynchronous teledermatology allows patients to submit clinical photographs through mobile applications for dermatologist review within 24-48 hours, significantly reducing wait times for specialist referral. Remote patient monitoring (RPM) programs continuously collect vital signs including blood pressure, pulse oximetry, blood glucose, and weight through Bluetooth-enabled medical devices that transmit readings to cloud-based electronic health record (EHR) systems. Chronic care management protocols for diabetes, hypertension, congestive heart failure, and COPD use RPM data to trigger automated clinical alerts when physiological parameters exceed individualized thresholds. The Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) reimburse telehealth encounters under CPT codes 99201-99215 for evaluation and management services, with RPM services billed under CPT 99453, 99454, and 99457. Telehealth adoption accelerated dramatically during the COVID-19 pandemic, with virtual visit volumes increasing over 3000% compared to pre-pandemic baselines. Integration with pharmacy networks enables electronic prescribing (e-prescribing) of medications including Schedule II-V controlled substances through state-approved telehealth prescribing frameworks.
