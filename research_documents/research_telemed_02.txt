Wearable biosensors integrated with telehealth infrastructure enable continuous physiological monitoring outside traditional clinical settings, transforming chronic disease management from episodic to proactive care delivery. Photoplethysmography (PPG) sensors in smartwatches detect irregular heart rhythms including atrial fibrillation with sensitivity exceeding 94%, prompting cardiologist teleconsultation for confirmatory electrocardiogram interpretation. Continuous glucose monitors (CGMs) such as Dexcom G7 and Abbott FreeStyle Libre transmit interstitial glucose readings every 5 minutes to smartphone applications, allowing endocrinologists to remotely adjust insulin dosing through telehealth portals. Implantable cardiac monitors like the Medtronic LINQ transmit arrhythmia episodes and heart failure hemodynamic data through cellular gateways to remote monitoring centers staffed by cardiac device nurses. Pulmonary rehabilitation programs deploy home spirometry devices that measure forced expiratory volume (FEV1) and peak expiratory flow (PEF), with readings reviewed by pulmonologists during scheduled telehealth appointments. Mental health platforms utilize ecological momentary assessment (EMA) through smartphone-delivered questionnaires (PHQ-9, GAD-7) to track depression and anxiety symptom trajectories between telepsychiatry sessions. Data interoperability between wearable platforms and EHR systems relies on HL7 FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) APIs to standardize clinical data exchange. Patient-generated health data (PGHD) governance frameworks address consent, data ownership, and secondary research use under HIPAA Privacy Rule provisions.
