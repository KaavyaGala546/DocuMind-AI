Quantum algorithms offer provable exponential or polynomial speedups for specific computational problems intractable on classical hardware. Shor's factoring algorithm decomposes large integers into prime factors in polynomial time using quantum Fourier transforms, directly threatening RSA-2048 encryption. Grover's unstructured search algorithm provides quadratic speedup, reducing database search from O(N) to O(sqrt(N)) oracle queries. Quantum simulation of molecular Hamiltonians using Suzuki-Trotter decomposition and qubitization enables accurate prediction of electronic ground-state energies for drug discovery and catalyst design. The quantum linear systems algorithm (HHL) solves systems of linear equations exponentially faster than classical methods, with applications in machine learning and finite element analysis. Quantum walk algorithms accelerate graph traversal problems including element distinctness and triangle finding. Quantum machine learning algorithms leverage amplitude encoding to represent exponentially large feature vectors in logarithmic qubit registers, enabling quantum kernel methods and quantum support vector machines. Variational quantum circuits with parameterized rotation gates serve as quantum neural networks, optimized through classical gradient descent on expectation value landscapes. Post-quantum cryptography standardization by NIST has selected lattice-based schemes (CRYSTALS-Kyber, CRYSTALS-Dilithium) to replace vulnerable RSA and elliptic curve systems.
