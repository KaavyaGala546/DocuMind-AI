Zero-trust security architectures eliminate implicit trust boundaries by enforcing continuous authentication and micro-segmentation across all network layers. Every access request undergoes identity verification through multi-factor authentication combining TOTP tokens, FIDO2 hardware keys, and biometric validation regardless of the request origin. Network micro-segmentation divides the infrastructure into granular security zones using software-defined perimeters, preventing lateral movement after initial compromise. Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) platforms aggregate logs from firewalls, intrusion detection systems (IDS), and endpoint detection and response (EDR) agents to correlate threat indicators. Extended Detection and Response (XDR) solutions unify telemetry across email gateways, cloud workloads, and network traffic for comprehensive threat visibility. The MITRE ATT&CK framework maps adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) across the kill chain from initial reconnaissance through privilege escalation to data exfiltration. Security orchestration and automated response (SOAR) playbooks automate incident triage, containment, and remediation workflows to reduce mean time to respond (MTTR). Privileged access management (PAM) systems enforce just-in-time elevation, session recording, and credential vaulting to minimize the blast radius of compromised administrator accounts. Deception technology deploys honeypots and honeytokens throughout production environments to detect adversaries who bypass perimeter defenses.
